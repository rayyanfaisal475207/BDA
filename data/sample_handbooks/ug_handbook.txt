UG STUDENT HANDBOOK

ACADEMIC POLICIES AND PROCEDURES

1. ADMISSION AND REGISTRATION

1.1 Minimum GPA Requirement
The minimum cumulative GPA required for continuing studies is 2.0. A student whose GPA falls below 2.0 may be placed on academic probation. The student is expected to improve their GPA to at least 2.0 within the next semester. If the GPA remains below 2.0 after two consecutive semesters, the student may be dismissed from the program.

1.2 Course Registration
All students must register for courses before the start of the semester. Late registration is not permitted without approval from the Academic Dean. Students must maintain a full-time status by registering for a minimum of 12 credit hours per semester.

1.3 Minimum and Maximum Credit Hours
The minimum credit hours required per semester is 12. The maximum credit hours is 18 unless special permission is granted by the Academic Dean. Students may petition for more than 18 credit hours if they have maintained a GPA of 3.5 or above.

2. ACADEMIC PERFORMANCE AND GRADING

2.1 Grading Scale
The following grading scale is used:
A: 90-100 (4.0)
B: 80-89 (3.0)
C: 70-79 (2.0)
D: 60-69 (1.0)
F: Below 60 (0.0)

2.2 GPA Calculation
The GPA is calculated as the weighted average of all course grades taken at the institution. The GPA is calculated on a 4.0 scale.

2.3 Grade Appeal
Students may appeal a grade within 30 days of receiving their grade report. The appeal must be submitted in writing to the instructor. If the student is not satisfied with the instructor's response, they may appeal to the Department Chair.

3. ATTENDANCE POLICY

3.1 Class Attendance
Regular attendance is mandatory. Students are expected to attend all classes. Instructors have the right to award a failing grade to students with excessive absences.

3.2 Absence Reporting
Students must inform their instructors of any unavoidable absences. Medical certificates should be submitted if absent due to illness. Three consecutive unauthorized absences may result in automatic withdrawal from the course.

3.3 Make-up Classes
Make-up classes will be arranged for absences due to legitimate reasons such as illness, family emergencies, or university-sponsored events.

4. COURSE REQUIREMENTS AND CREDIT SYSTEM

4.1 Credit Hours
Each course has a certain number of credit hours based on the number of lectures and lab hours. One credit hour is equivalent to one hour of lecture per week for a semester. Laboratory courses carry 0.5 credit hours per lab hour.

4.2 Course Repetition
A student may repeat a course in which they received a D or F grade. The repeated course grade will replace the previous grade in the GPA calculation. The course may only be repeated once.

4.3 Prerequisite Requirements
Students must have completed the prerequisite courses with a minimum grade of C before enrolling in higher-level courses. Exceptions must be approved by the Department Chair.

5. ACADEMIC PROBATION AND DISMISSAL

5.1 Academic Probation Criteria
A student is placed on academic probation if their cumulative GPA falls below 2.0. The student is expected to improve their GPA to at least 2.0 within one semester.

5.2 Dismissal Policy
If a student's cumulative GPA remains below 2.0 after two consecutive semesters on probation, the student may be dismissed from the program. A dismissed student may appeal to the Academic Senate.

5.3 Reinstatement
A dismissed student may request reinstatement after sitting out for one full year. The student must submit a formal appeal explaining how they plan to succeed if reinstated.

6. DEGREE REQUIREMENTS

6.1 General Degree Requirements
To earn a bachelor's degree, a student must complete a minimum of 120 credit hours with a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0. Students must also meet all major and elective requirements.

6.2 Major Requirements
Each major has specific course requirements that must be completed. These requirements typically include 40-60 credit hours in the major field of study.

6.3 General Education Requirements
All students must complete general education requirements including English composition, mathematics, sciences, humanities, and social sciences. These typically account for 30-40 credit hours.

7. GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS

7.1 Eligibility for Graduation
Students are eligible for graduation when they have completed all degree requirements and have a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0. A final review is conducted to ensure all requirements have been met.

7.2 Graduation Application
Students must apply for graduation by the deadline specified by the Registrar's Office. Late applications may result in delayed graduation.

7.3 Academic Honors
Students who graduate with a GPA of 3.5 or above graduate with honors. Those with a GPA of 3.8 or above graduate with highest honors.
