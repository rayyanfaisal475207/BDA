NUST SEECS POSTGRADUATE STUDENT HANDBOOK
School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
National University of Sciences and Technology
Islamabad, Pakistan

PART I — ADMISSION TO POSTGRADUATE PROGRAMMES

1.1 MS Programme Admission
Candidates applying for Master of Science (MS) programmes must hold a four-year bachelor's degree in a relevant field with a minimum CGPA of 2.5 on a 4.0 scale (or 60% in aggregate for older grading systems). The candidate must also appear in the NUST Graduate Admission Test (GAT) and the department-specific written test/interview. HEC-approved GAT-General score of 50 or above is required.

1.2 PhD Programme Admission
Candidates for the Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) programme must hold an MS degree in a relevant field with a minimum CGPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale. PhD applicants undergo a written test, research proposal evaluation, and a formal interview before a departmental selection panel. Existing MS students at NUST may apply for direct MS-to-PhD upgrade after completing at least 24 credit hours with a CGPA of 3.5 or above.

1.3 MPhil Programme
The MPhil programme is for candidates who hold a 16-year academic background (bachelor's plus postgraduate diploma or equivalent). Admission criteria are similar to MS, but the programme focus is research-oriented with course-work in the first year followed by a thesis.

1.4 International and Overseas Pakistanis
International students and overseas Pakistanis must submit IBCC equivalence for foreign degrees. English proficiency test scores (TOEFL iBT minimum 79 or IELTS minimum 6.0) are required for students from non-English medium backgrounds.

PART II — REGISTRATION AND COURSE LOAD

2.1 Course Registration
Postgraduate students must register for courses at the start of each semester through the NUST student portal. Registration must be completed within the first two weeks. Late registration attracts a PKR 2,000 per-day fee.

2.2 Credit Hours Per Semester
MS and MPhil students may register for a maximum of 12 credit hours per semester and a minimum of 6 credit hours. PhD students in the course-work phase register for a minimum of 6 credit hours. During the research phase (after qualifying examination), PhD students register for a research-hours code only.

2.3 Full-Time vs Part-Time Status
A student registered for 9 or more credit hours is considered full-time. A student registered for 6–8 credit hours is part-time. Part-time students may take longer to complete the programme but must still complete within the maximum allowed duration.

PART III — ACADEMIC REQUIREMENTS

3.1 MS Degree Requirements
To earn an MS degree at NUST, a student must:
(a) Complete a minimum of 30 credit hours.
(b) Achieve a minimum CGPA of 2.5 throughout the programme.
(c) Complete 18 credit hours of coursework with a minimum CGPA of 2.5.
(d) Complete an MS Thesis (6 credit hours) or an MS Project (6 credit hours) with at least one publication or an equivalent research deliverable.
(e) Pass the thesis defence (viva voce) before the department's thesis evaluation committee.

3.2 PhD Degree Requirements
To earn a PhD degree, a student must:
(a) Complete a minimum of 18 credit hours of coursework (beyond the MS degree, if relevant).
(b) Pass a Qualifying Examination (QE) after completing coursework.
(c) Submit and defend a Research Proposal (RP) for doctoral candidacy.
(d) Complete original doctoral research and submit a dissertation.
(e) Publish a minimum of one research paper in an HEC-recognised W-category journal or present at an internationally recognised conference.
(f) Pass the final PhD dissertation defence before an examination committee that includes at least one external evaluator.

3.3 Grading Scale (Postgraduate)
The same 4.0 grading scale used at undergraduate level applies. A grade of C (2.0) is the minimum passing grade for postgraduate courses. A grade of C- or below is a failing grade and the course must be repeated. MS students must maintain a minimum CGPA of 2.5.

3.4 Thesis and Dissertation

3.4.1 MS Thesis
The MS Thesis is an original research contribution of approximately 60–100 pages. The student must identify a thesis supervisor from the NUST faculty before the start of the second semester. The Thesis Evaluation Committee consists of the supervisor, a co-supervisor (optional), and at least one external examiner.

3.4.2 PhD Dissertation
The PhD dissertation must represent a substantial and original contribution to knowledge. It typically spans 150–250 pages. The evaluation committee must include at least two external reviewers, one of whom must be from an internationally recognised institution.

3.5 Qualifying Examination (PhD)
PhD students must pass the Qualifying Examination within two semesters of completing their required coursework. The QE has a written component and an oral component. A student who fails the QE may re-sit once. Failure in the second attempt leads to withdrawal from the PhD programme.

PART IV — SUPERVISION AND RESEARCH

4.1 Supervisor Assignment
Every MS and PhD student must have a formally appointed supervisor from the NUST faculty before the end of the first semester. The supervisor is responsible for guiding the student's academic progress and research. Students may request a change of supervisor for valid reasons, subject to departmental approval.

4.2 Research Proposal
MS and PhD students must submit a formal Research Proposal within the second semester for MS, and within the first year for PhD. The proposal must be reviewed and approved by the supervisor and the departmental research committee. PhD students must defend their proposal orally before the committee.

4.3 Progress Reports
Postgraduate research students must submit a bi-annual (every six months) Progress Report to their supervisor and the department. Supervisors forward these reports to the Graduate Studies Office. Unsatisfactory progress may result in probation or termination of enrolment.

4.4 Research Ethics
All postgraduate research involving human subjects, animals, or sensitive data must obtain clearance from the NUST Institutional Review Board (IRB) before commencing. Failure to obtain IRB clearance is grounds for nullification of research.

PART V — ATTENDANCE AND CONDUCT

5.1 Attendance Policy
The same attendance policy applicable to undergraduate students applies to postgraduate students. Students may not exceed 20% absences in any course. A student who exceeds this threshold receives an automatic failing grade in that course.

5.2 Academic Integrity
Academic integrity standards are strictly enforced at the postgraduate level. Plagiarism, duplicate publication, data fabrication, and falsification are serious research misconduct offences investigated by the Graduate Studies Office and the Academic Integrity Committee. Sanctions range from grade failure to permanent expulsion and may include notification to the student's funding body.

PART VI — SCHOLARSHIPS AND FUNDING

6.1 NUST Postgraduate Scholarship
Meritorious MS students may be awarded a full-tuition scholarship and monthly stipend, subject to availability of funds. Recipients must maintain a minimum CGPA of 3.0 and make satisfactory research progress. Loss of good academic standing terminates the scholarship.

6.2 HEC Scholarships
The Higher Education Commission (HEC) of Pakistan offers several scholarship schemes for postgraduate students including:
- Indigenous PhD Fellowships (full funding for PhD students)
- Overseas Scholarships (for PhD studies abroad)
- HRDI Faculty Development Programme
Students must apply independently through the HEC portal. NUST provides all necessary documentation and support letters.

6.3 Research Grants
Faculty supervisors may fund graduate students through research grants. Funded students are typically required to contribute a fixed number of research hours per week as per the grant agreement.

PART VII — TIME LIMITS AND EXTENSIONS

7.1 Maximum Duration — MS/MPhil
MS and MPhil students must complete all degree requirements within four years of initial enrolment. Students who fail to complete within this period may apply for a one-year extension. The extension application must be submitted before the expiry of the four-year limit and requires strong justification and supervisor endorsement.

7.2 Maximum Duration — PhD
PhD students must complete all degree requirements within six years of initial enrolment. A one-year extension may be applied for before the six-year period expires. Students funded by HEC or NUST grants must adhere to the grant timeline regardless of the general policy.

7.3 Leave of Absence
A postgraduate student may apply for a leave of absence (semester freeze) for compelling personal, medical, or professional reasons. A maximum of two semesters of leave may be taken during an MS programme, and a maximum of four semesters during a PhD programme. The time spent on leave does not count toward the maximum programme duration.

PART VIII — GRADUATION

8.1 Graduation Eligibility
A student is eligible to graduate upon satisfying all academic requirements and having no outstanding financial obligations or disciplinary actions. The Graduation Audit is conducted by the Registrar's Office in the penultimate semester.

8.2 Graduation Application
Students must file a Graduation Application through the NUST student portal by the end of the fourth week of the graduating semester. Students who fail to file on time are deferred to the next graduation cycle.

8.3 Posthumous Degree
In the unfortunate event of the death of a student who was in the final stages of completing degree requirements, NUST may award a posthumous degree at the discretion of the Academic Council upon the petition of the family.

APPENDIX B — SAMPLE POSTGRADUATE QUERIES

Q: What is the minimum CGPA to continue in an MS programme?
A: MS students must maintain a minimum CGPA of 2.5. Falling below this threshold places the student on academic probation.

Q: How many credit hours are required for an MS degree?
A: A minimum of 30 credit hours, including 18 in coursework and 6 for thesis/project (plus remaining electives).

Q: What is the maximum duration allowed for completing an MS degree?
A: Four years. A one-year extension may be requested before expiry.

Q: How do I qualify for a PhD programme?
A: A minimum MS CGPA of 3.0, passing the departmental written test and interview, along with an HEC-approved GAT score.

Q: Can I change my PhD supervisor?
A: Yes, students may request a change of supervisor for valid reasons. Departmental approval is required.

Q: What publications are required for a PhD degree?
A: At least one research paper in an HEC W-category journal or an internationally recognised conference is required.

Q: What is the Qualifying Examination for PhD students?
A: The QE is a written and oral examination conducted after coursework. It may be attempted twice. Failure in the second attempt leads to withdrawal.
